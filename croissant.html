How to Make Croissant

Materials:

  • 200 grams high-protein flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 100 ml warm milk
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 100 grams cold butter (for lamination)
  • 1 egg yolk + 1 tablespoon milk (mixed, for egg wash)

Steps:

  1. First, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Next, add the warm milk and melted butter, then knead until the dough comes together and becomes smooth.
  3. Then, cover the dough with a clean cloth and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  4. While the dough is rising, place the cold butter between two sheets of plastic wrap or baking paper.
  5. Using a rolling pin, flatten the butter into a thin square (about 10x10 cm).
  6. Place in the refrigerator to keep it cold.
  7. Once the dough has risen, roll it out into a rectangle. Place the cold butter square in the center.
  8. Fold the sides of the dough over the butter, completely covering it.
  9. Now, roll the dough lengthwise, then fold it into thirds.
  10. Cover and chill in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to prevent the butter from melting.
  11. Repeat this folding and chilling process two more times to create flaky layers.
  12. After the final chill, roll the dough out to a thickness of about ½ cm. Cut into long triangles.
  13. Starting from the base of each triangle, roll the dough towards the tip to form a croissant shape.
  14. Place the croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and let them rise for 1 hour.
  15. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 180°C.
  16. Brush the surface of the croissants with the egg yolk and milk mixture.
  17. Carefully bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.
  18. Finally, let the croissants cool slightly and enjoy with jam, chocolate spread, or extra butter.

    Tips:

  • Use high-quality butter for a more buttery and tender croissant.
  • Always chill the dough between folds to maintain the layers.
  • Avoid pressing the dough too hard when rolling to preserve the layers.
