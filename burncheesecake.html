How to Make Burn Cheesecake

Materials:

  • 250 grams of cream cheese (room temperature)
  • 80 grams of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 125 ml of whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • ½ tablespoon of lemon juice

Steps:

  1. First, preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Next, prepare a baking pan. Line it with crumpled parchment paper for a more rustic look.
  3. Now, in a mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  4. Then, add the eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed after each addition.
  5. After that, stir in the whipping cream, vanilla extract, and lemon juice. Mix until well combined.
  6. Sift in the flour and gently fold until just combined and there are no lumps.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven at 200°C for 30-40 minutes, or until the top is deeply browned and looks "burnt."
  9. For a creamier center, reduce the baking time by about 5 minutes.
  10. Allow the cheesecake to cool to room temperature before refrigerating it for at least 4 hours to allow the texture to set.
  11. Finally, cut into slices and serve. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar or top with fresh fruit.

    Tips:

  • Use high-quality cream cheese for the best results.
  • Avoid overmixing the batter to keep the cheesecake tender.
  • For a more caramelized flavor, bake for a longer time until the surface is darker.
