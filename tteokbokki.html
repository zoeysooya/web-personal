How to Make Tteokbokki

Materials:

    Main Ingredients:

  • 100 grams of tteok (Korean rice cakes)
  • 250 ml of water or anchovy/chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil (for stir-frying)
  • 2 fish balls (optional, cut in half)
  • ½ stalk of green onion (sliced diagonally)
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds (for garnish)

    • Gochujang Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • ½ tablespoon of chili sauce (optional, for extra spiciness)
  • ½ tablespoon of ketchup
  • ½ tablespoon of soy sauce
  • ½ tablespoon of sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of Korean chili powder (gochugaru) (optional)

Steps:

  1. First, if using hard, instant tteok, soak them in warm water for 15 minutes to soften them.
  2. However, if using fresh tteok, you can cook them immediately without soaking.
  3. Next, in a small bowl, combine the gochujang, chili sauce, ketchup, soy sauce, sugar, salt, and Korean chili powder (if using).
  4. Mix all ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
  5. Then, heat sesame oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté the green onion until fragrant.
  6. Pour in the water or broth, then add the prepared gochujang sauce.
  7. Stir well and bring to a boil
  8. Add the tteok and fish balls, then gently stir until the sauce thickens (about 5-7 minutes).
  9. Remove the tteokbokki from the heat and serve on a plate.
  10. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and additional sliced green onions.
  11. Optionally, you can add a boiled egg or mozzarella cheese on top for extra flavor.
  12. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water while cooking.
  13. Add sausage, fish cake, or odeng for flavor variations.
  14. For a creamier version, add a little milk while cooking.
