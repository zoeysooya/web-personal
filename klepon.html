How to Make Klepon

Materials:

  • 150 grams of glutinous rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon of rice flour (for a chewier texture)
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of pandan paste (or pandan leaf extract)
  • 100 ml of warm water
  • 50 grams of palm sugar (finely grated)
  • 100 grams of grated coconut
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt (for the coconut mixture)

Steps:

  1. First, combine the glutinous rice flour, rice flour, salt, and pandan paste in a mixing bowl.
  2. Gradually add warm water little by little, kneading slowly until the dough is smooth and pliable.
  3. Then, take a small amount of dough, flatten it, and fill it with grated palm sugar.
  4. Use soft palm sugar so it melts more easily when cooked.
  5. Next, close the dough and shape it into a neat ball. Repeat until all the dough is used up.
  6. Make sure the Klepon dough is tightly sealed so the palm sugar doesn't leak during boiling.
  7. After that, boil water in a pot, then carefully add the Klepon.
  8. Cook until the Klepon float to the surface (a sign that they are cooked), then immediately remove and drain them.
  9. Add pandan leaves to the boiling water if you want a more fragrant flavor.
  10. Steam the grated coconut mixed with salt for 10 minutes to prevent it from spoiling quickly.
  11. Finally, roll the Klepon in the grated coconut until evenly coated.
  12. Klepon is now ready to be served with a chewy texture and a melting palm sugar filling.
