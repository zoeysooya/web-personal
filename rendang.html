How to Make Rendang

Materials:

    Main Ingredients:

  • 500 grams of beef (cut into cubes, not too small)
  • 500 ml of thick coconut milk (from 1 coconut)
  • 500 ml of thin coconut milk
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 lemongrass stalks (bruised)
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 cm of galangal (bruised)
  • 1 turmeric leaf (knotted)
  • Salt to taste

    • Ground Spices:

  • 6 shallots
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 4 candlenuts (roasted)
  • 2 cm of ginger
  • 2 cm of turmeric
  • 5 large red chilies
  • 5 red bird's eye chilies (optional, for extra spiciness)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander

Steps:

  1. First, heat a little oil in a wok or large pan. Make sure the pan is large enough to hold all the ingredients.
  2. Then, saut√© the ground spices with the lemongrass, bay leaves, kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaf, and galangal until fragrant and cooked through. Stir frequently to prevent burning.
  3. Next, add the beef cubes and stir until they change color.
  4. Slowly pour in the thin coconut milk little by little, while stirring gently to prevent the coconut milk from separating.
  5. After that, cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the coconut milk starts to thicken and the oil begins to separate.
  6. Now, add the thick coconut milk and continue cooking over low heat until the rendang dries out and turns dark brown.
  7. Finally, remove the rendang from the heat and serve with warm rice and fresh vegetables.

    Tips:

  • Always use low heat and cook slowly for tender rendang and perfectly infused spices.
  • Don't stir too often to prevent the meat from falling apart.
  • Remember the longer it cooks, the tastier it becomes as the spices are more deeply absorbed.
  • Adjust the amount of chili to your taste for the level of spiciness.
