How to Make Es Cendol

Materials:

    Main Ingredients:

  • 100 grams rice flour
  • 50 grams tapioca flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pandan paste (or 5 pandan leaves blended with 200 ml water, then strained)
  • 500 ml water
  • Ice cubes, as needed

    • Coconut Milk Sauce:

  • 500 ml coconut milk (from 1 coconut)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 pandan leaves (knotted)

    • Palm Sugar Sauce:

  • 200 grams palm sugar (finely grated)
  • 100 ml water
  • 1 pandan leaf

Steps:

  1. Firstly, combine the rice flour, tapioca flour, salt, and pandan paste in a saucepan.
  2. Pour in the water. Then, stir well until there are no lumps.
  3. Next, cook over low heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and forms a gel. Be careful not to burn it.
  4. Prepare a bowl of ice water.
  5. After that, using a cendol strainer or a perforated plastic bag, press the cendol dough into the ice water.
  6. Then, allow the cendol to harden in the ice water, then drain. You should do this quickly, so it doesn't melt.
  7. To make the coconut milk sauce, boil the coconut milk with salt and pandan leaves, stirring occasionally to prevent curdling.
  8. Always use fresh coconut milk for a richer and creamier taste.
  9. If desired, add a little vanilla to the coconut milk sauce for a more fragrant aroma.
  10. After it boils, turn off the heat and let it cool.
  11. For the palm sugar sauce, boil the palm sugar with water and a pandan leaf until dissolved.
  12. Strain to remove impurities. After that, let it cool.
  13. To assemble, prepare a glass.
  14. Put the cendol in the glass.
  15. Next, add ice cubes.
  16. Pour in the coconut milk and liquid palm sugar.
  17. Stir well before drinking to ensure the flavors are evenly distributed.
  18. Finally, Es Cendol is ready to be served. It tastes better if it is served cold.
  19. Es cendol is best enjoyed cold and consumed immediately.
