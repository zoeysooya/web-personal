How to Make Sate Ayam

Materials:

    Main Ingredients:

  • 250 grams of boneless chicken (thigh or breast)
  • 10 bamboo skewers (soak in water to prevent burning)

    • Marinade Ingredients:

  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon of sweet soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of ground pepper
  • ½ teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon of cooking oil

    Peanut Sauce Ingredients:

  • 100 grams of peanuts (roasted and ground)
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 red chilies (optional, adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar (shaved)
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of chicken bouillon powder (optional)
  • 200 ml of water
  • 1 tablespoon of sweet soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon of lime juice (for a fresh aroma)

Steps:

  1. First, cut the chicken into small cubes.
  2. Next, mix the chicken with minced garlic, sweet soy sauce, salt, pepper, ground coriander, and cooking oil in a bowl.
  3. Then, stir well to combine thoroughly and leave it to marinate for at least 30 minutes. This really helps the flavors meld.
  4. After that, sauté the garlic and red chilies in a pan until fragrant.
  5. Now, add the ground peanuts, water, palm sugar, salt, and chicken bouillon powder (if using).
  6. Continue to cook the sauce until it thickens, then add the sweet soy sauce and lime juice.
  7. Take the marinated chicken and thread it onto the bamboo skewers.
  8. Grill the satay over charcoal or on a Teflon pan, occasionally brushing with peanut sauce and sweet soy sauce.
  9. Flip the satay frequently until cooked through and lightly browned.
  10. Finally, serve the chicken satay with peanut sauce, lontong (rice cake), and sliced shallots. You can add sweet soy sauce with chili and crackers for a more delicious sensation.
