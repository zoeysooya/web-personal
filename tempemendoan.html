How to Make Tempe Mendoan

Materials:

  • 1 tempeh board (cut thinly and widely)
  • Cooking oil (enough for frying)
  • 100 grams of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1 stalk of green onion (finely sliced)
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ teaspoon of powdered broth (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 clove of garlic (minced)
  • ½ teaspoon of turmeric powder (for golden color)
  • 150 ml of water

Steps:

  1. First, combine all-purpose flour, rice flour, salt, powdered broth, ground coriander, minced garlic, and turmeric powder in a mixing bowl.
  2. Then, slowly add water little by little while stirring until the batter reaches a moderately thick consistency. Stir gently to avoid lumps.
  3. Next, add the sliced green onions to the batter and mix well.
  4. After that, heat enough oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Make sure the oil is hot enough before frying.
  5. Now, dip the tempeh slices into the batter, ensuring they are fully coated on all sides.
  6. Carefully place the battered tempeh into the hot oil.
  7. Fry until the outside is slightly crispy but still soft on the inside (about 2-3 minutes per side). Do not overcook to maintain its tenderness.
  8. Subsequently, remove the fried tempeh and drain it on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  9. Finally, serve the Tempe Mendoan with soy sauce with sliced chili or fresh chili for a spicy sensation.

    Tips:

  • Always monitor the frying process to prevent burning.
  • If you desire a crispier texture, consider adding a pinch of baking powder to the batter.
  • Optionally, add sliced galangal for a traditional mendoan aroma.
