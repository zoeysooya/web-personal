How to Make Spaghetti Carbonara

Materials:

  • 100 grams of spaghetti
  • 50 grams of smoked beef or pancetta (thinly sliced)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 25 grams of Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • 1 clove of garlic (finely chopped)
  • 50 ml of fresh cream or milk
  • ½ tablespoon of butter
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. First, boil the spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente (about 8-10 minutes).
  2. Next, drain the spaghetti, reserving a little pasta water.
  3. In a bowl, combine the egg yolk and Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  4. Then, add a pinch of black pepper for extra flavor.
  5. Now, melt the butter in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  6. Add the smoked beef or pancetta and cook until it's browned and slightly crispy.
  7. Remove the pan from the heat. Quickly add the cooked spaghetti to the pan.
  8. Pour the egg yolk and cheese mixture over the spaghetti. Immediately toss to coat the spaghetti evenly. If the sauce is too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water to reach the desired consistency.
  9. Season with salt and black pepper to taste
  10. Serve the spaghetti carbonara on a plate and sprinkle with chopped parsley and extra Parmesan cheese.
  11. Finally, add extra black pepper, if you like.
  12. Make sure the heat is off when you mix the sauce to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  13. Use authentic Parmesan cheese for a more authentic flavor.
